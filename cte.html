Computer Science and Technology: This course introduces students to computer programming, software development, and technology infrastructure. Students learn programming languages, software engineering principles, and network management skills.

Engineering and Design: In this course, students explore various engineering disciplines such as mechanical, electrical, and civil engineering. They learn design principles, CAD (Computer-Aided Design), and participate in hands-on projects.

Health Sciences: Health Sciences courses offer students an introduction to healthcare professions. Students learn about anatomy, physiology, medical terminology, and healthcare ethics. They may also have opportunities for clinical experience or internships.

Culinary Arts: Culinary Arts courses focus on food preparation, kitchen safety, nutrition, and culinary techniques. Students learn cooking skills, menu planning, and hospitality management. Practical experience may include working in a school kitchen or restaurant setting.

Automotive Technology: This course provides instruction in automotive repair and maintenance. Students learn about engine systems, electrical systems, and automotive diagnostics. Hands-on experience is emphasized, with opportunities to work on vehicles and gain industry certifications.

Construction Trades: Construction Trades courses cover topics such as carpentry, plumbing, electrical work, and construction management. Students learn practical skills related to building construction, renovation, and blueprint reading. Safety protocols and industry standards are also emphasized.

Graphic Design and Multimedia: In this course, students explore graphic design principles, digital media production, and visual communication. They learn software tools like Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator, as well as techniques for creating digital artwork, animations, and multimedia presentations.

Business and Entrepreneurship: Business and Entrepreneurship courses focus on fundamental business concepts such as marketing, finance, management, and entrepreneurship. Students learn about business planning, market research, and financial literacy. They may also develop and pitch their own business ideas.

These CTE courses at Thomas A. Edison CTE High School provide students with valuable skills and knowledge in diverse career pathways, preparing them for further education or entry into the workforce upon graduation.
